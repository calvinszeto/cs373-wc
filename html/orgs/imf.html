Summary

The IMF was created on July 22, 1944 at the Bretton Woods Conference with the intention of rebuilding the global economic system after WWII. The IMF was formally organized on December 27, 1945 when 29 countries signed the Articles of Agreement. The IMF functions like a bank and makes sure that borrowing states can pay their debts on time. The IMF also offers member nations information on economic policies of all member nations, financial support in times of economic difficulties, and increased opportunities for trade and investment. The IMF is one of the key organizations of the international economic system. Currently, the IMF has 188 member nations and its largest borrowers are Greece, Portugal, Ireland, Romania, and Ukraine.
