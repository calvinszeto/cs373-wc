Greece Debt Crisis

Greece was living beyond its means before adopting the Euro and after adopting the Euro public spending significantly rose. After years of overspending, Greece’s budget deficit spiraled out of control, which left Greece unprepared to handle the global economic downturn. Greece’s debt reached levels so high that it was unable to pay, so required massive loans from European partners and the IMF
