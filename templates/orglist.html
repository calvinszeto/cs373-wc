International Monetary Fund

The IMF was created on July 22, 1944 at the Bretton Woods Conference with the intention of rebuilding the global economic system after WWII. The IMF was formally organized on December 27, 1945 when 29 countries signed the Articles of Agreement. The IMF functions like a bank and makes sure that borrowing states can pay their debts on time. The IMF also offers member nations information on economic policies of all member nations, financial support in times of economic difficulties, and increased opportunities for trade and investment. The IMF is one of the key organizations of the international economic system. Currently, the IMF has 188 member nations and its largest borrowers are Greece, Portugal, Ireland, Romania, and Ukraine.

The American Academy of Pediatrics

Founded in 1930 by 35 pediatricians. The AAP has produced more than 480 publications in 140 countries. The AAP and its members work at the local level in hospitals, offices and clinics and in state and federal government to protect the well being of children.

BP

British multinational oil and gas company which is the third-largest energy company and fourth-largest company in the world measured by 2011 revenues. It is vertically integrated and is active in every area of the oil and gas industry, including exploration and production,refining, distribution and marketing, petrochemicals, power generation and trading. It also has renewable energy activities in biofuelsand wind power. Its largest division is BP America, which is the second-largest producer of oil and gas in the United States and is headquartered in Houston, Texas. As of December 2011, BP had total proven commercial reserves of 17.75 billion barrels of oil equivalent. The name "BP" derives from the initials of one of the company's former legal names, British Petroleum.
